Ang Probinsyudad

Taguig is a city in the southeastern part of the capital region, known for its rich history and vibrant culture. It has undergone significant transformation in recent years and is now a hub for business, commerce, and entertainment. The city is home to popular destinations such as Bonifacio Global City, yet it still retains some of its provincial charm. Taguig has a deep connection to its roots as a rural fishing and agricultural town, and its diverse and welcoming community embraces both tradition and innovation.

Parola

This centuries-old lighthouse became the secret rendezvous point of the Katipunan as it planned its revolution for Philippine Independence. The Parola was chosen by KKK Supremo Gat Andres Bonifacio as a meeting place because it was the most accessible channel from Tondo, Manila and from the Municipios along the Pasig River and the Province of Laguna. This site is one of the remaining provincial charms of the city.

St. Anne Church

Built in 1587, this church, located in Barangay Sta. Ana, Taguig, is definitely one of the oldest churches in the Philippines. St. Anne Church is also one of the standing historical establishments remaining in the city.

Libingan ng mga Bayani

The Libingan ng mga Bayani or the Cemetery of the Heroes is the final resting place of 33,520 Filipinos who laid their lives for the country during the Second World War. Dignitaries and personnel of the Philippine military from privates to generals are also buried here. The Republic Memorial Cemetery at Fort McKinley was established in May 1947 as a fitting tribute to the gallant Filipino men and women who brought honor to the country and fought for the sake of freedom and democracy. This was intended for National Shrine purposes and placed under the administration of the Military Shrines Services, Philippine Veterans Affairs, Department of National Defense.

Veterans Museum

The Veteran's Museum in Taguig showcases World War II through life-sized montages and various art forms combined with advanced technology, and includes a secret tunnel used during the war. The museum displays war materials and dioramas depicting the multi-dimensional role of army soldiers.

Taguig Lakeshore Hall

The home of Mercado del Lago, a floating food park by the lake, the Taguig Lakeshore Hall is a versatile and elegant event venue that offers stunning views of the city's lake and state-of-the-art facilities. It is an ideal location for weddings, corporate events, and other special occasions, providing guests with a memorable and unforgettable experience.

Venice Grand Canal Mall

The Venice Grand Canal Mall is a premier lifestyle destination located in the McKinley Hill development in Taguig City. Inspired by the romantic Italian city of Venice, the mall features stunning architecture, picturesque canals, and a wide array of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

The Manila American Cemetery and Memorial

The Manila American Cemetery and Memorial is located in Taguig City and is the largest American cemetery in Asia with over 17,000 graves of soldiers who fought and died during World War II. The Memorial Chapel is the centerpiece of the site, featuring a large altar and stained glass windows depicting the American eagle and Philippine flag, as well as a mosaic mural honoring the American and Filipino soldiers who fought together during the war.

Bonifacio Global City

BGC is a bustling commercial and residential district in Taguig City, Philippines, formerly a military base. It features a dynamic skyline with some of the country's tallest buildings and is home to major companies, as well as a thriving arts and culture scene. BGC is also known for its high-end residential towers, shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Market! Market!

Market! Market! is a popular shopping mall located in Bonifacio Global City in Taguig City, Philippines. It is a vibrant and bustling retail and entertainment hub that attracts locals and tourists alike with its wide range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

The Mind Museum

The Mind Museum is an educational facility aimed at fostering the public understanding of science. It is located on a 1.2 hectare lot in the J. Y. Campos Park in Bonifacio Global City. It is a project of the non-profit Bonifacio Art Foundation, Inc. It is dedicated to providing an extraordinary educational experience that inspires the public understanding of science.
